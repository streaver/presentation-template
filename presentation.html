Anatomy

Every feature has a purpose.

Flat tail

Acts as a rudder when swimming and a warning drum when slapped on water — the original alarm system.

Self-sharpening teeth

Orange incisors grow continuously and sharpen against each other, staying razor-sharp without any maintenance.

Nictitating eyelid

A transparent third eyelid closes underwater, acting as built-in swimming goggles while keeping vision sharp.

Webbed hind feet

Generate powerful thrust underwater; on land, the same feet let beavers carry mud and debris efficiently.

Construction

How a beaver builds a dam.

PHASE 01 Site selection

The beaver reads stream current and width to choose a crossing point. Narrow, fast-moving sections are preferred — less material needed, more water backed up.

→
PHASE 02 Foundation

Logs are driven into the streambed vertically, then branches are woven horizontally between them. The skeleton can withstand enormous hydraulic pressure.

→
PHASE 03 Sealing & maintenance

Mud, leaves, and plant matter are packed into gaps from the upstream side. Beavers repair leaks immediately — sometimes working through the night after a storm.

Behavior
Social structure

A monogamous family unit.

Beavers live in small family colonies of 2–8 individuals, occupying the same lodge and dam system for generations.

Breeding pair One male and one female, bonded for life. They share all construction and foraging duties equally.
Kits 1–4 young born each spring. Born fully furred and with eyes open — they can swim within 24 hours.
Yearlings Previous year's offspring remain with the family, helping to maintain the dam and forage for winter stores.
Territory Up to 5 km of waterway, defended with castoreum scent markings renewed daily at mound stations along the bank.

Impact

What a beaver-built wetland actually does.

With beavers

  • Standing water raises the local water table year-round.
  • Slow-moving ponds cool water temperatures by 4–8°C.
  • Sediment traps remove 90%+ of agricultural runoff nutrients.
  • Dense riparian vegetation reduces wildfire spread speed.
  • 40+ bird, fish, and amphibian species colonise within 5 years.

Without beavers

  • Streams run fast and ephemeral — drying out by midsummer.
  • Water temperatures spike, stressing cold-water fish like trout.
  • Sediment and nutrients flush directly downstream unchecked.
  • Bare stream banks offer no wildfire buffer or wind protection.
  • Species diversity drops significantly; apex predators leave first.

Conservation

Bringing them back on purpose.

Beaver reintroduction is now recognised as one of the most cost-effective wetland restoration tools available — faster and cheaper than engineered solutions.

Beavers
Conservation
Approaches

Three frameworks, one goal.

Reintroduction Captive-bred or wild-caught beavers are released into prepared habitat. Used in UK, Netherlands, and the US Pacific Northwest with measurable success.
Coexistence tools Flow devices (Beaver Deceivers) and pond levellers let beavers dam without flooding roads or crops — redirecting, not removing.
Rewilding zones Large-scale riparian corridors are left unmanaged, letting beaver populations self-establish and expand naturally along entire river catchments.
Beavers
Conservation
Recovery stages

From local extinction to keystone status.

STAGE 01 Regionally extinct — hunted or excluded, no population remains in the catchment.
STAGE 02 Reintroduced — small founding group established in protected trial site.
STAGE 03 Self-sustaining — reproduction confirmed; population no longer needs supplementation.
STAGE 04 Dispersing — young beavers colonising new territories; range expanding naturally.
STAGE 05 Keystone status — full ecosystem engineering function restored; water table, biodiversity and fire resilience measurably improved.
